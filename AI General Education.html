Key concepts to lock in
Token
The smallest unit AI reads — roughly a word or part of a word. AI processes everything as tokens, not letters or sentences.
Context window
How much text AI can "see" at once. Like short-term memory — once it fills up, earlier content falls out.
Training
The process where AI learned from billions of words of text — books, websites, code. It happened once. It's not learning from your conversations.
Next-token prediction
The actual mechanism: AI predicts the most likely next word given everything before it. That's it. That's the engine.
Parameters
The billions of numbers inside an AI model that encode everything it "learned" during training. More ≠ always better.
Hallucination
When AI generates text that sounds confident but is factually wrong. It's not lying — it's pattern-completing without knowing it's wrong.

💡 Why prompting is a thinking skill, not a typing skill

A good prompt is really a well-structured thought. When you give AI a role, context, goal, format, and constraints — you're not following a formula. You're thinking clearly about what you actually want. The people who get the best results from AI aren't the ones who know the most tricks — they're the ones who think most precisely. That's an IE skill. Systems thinking applied to communication.

The five elements of any great prompt — works in every AI tool
Role
"You are a..." — sets the expertise level and perspective AI takes. Works in Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini, all of them.
Context
Who you are, what situation you're in, what you already know. AI has no background — you have to provide it.
Goal
What the output is actually for. Not just "explain X" but "explain X so I can use it to do Y."
Format
How you want the output structured — length, bullets vs prose, tone, level of detail. Explicitly stating this eliminates guessing.
Constraints
What NOT to do. "Don't use jargon." "No generic advice." "Under 200 words." Negative constraints cut noise dramatically.
Iteration
First response = draft. The real skill is the follow-up. "Make it shorter / more specific / aimed at X audience." Always push back.

The AI critical thinking framework — use this every time
High-stakes = verify
Medical, legal, financial, safety decisions. If being wrong has real consequences, always verify with authoritative sources.
Cite-worthy? Check it
AI can fabricate citations, statistics, and quotes that sound real. Never cite AI-generated facts in academic work without verifying.
Confident ≠ correct
AI delivers wrong answers in exactly the same confident tone as correct ones. Confidence is not evidence of accuracy.
Ask for uncertainty
Prompt AI to tell you how confident it is and where it might be wrong. "What don't you know about this?" is a powerful question.
Lateral reading
Don't verify a claim by asking AI again. Go to independent sources — original papers, official sites, established publications.
Your judgment > AI
AI doesn't have values, judgment, or stakes in outcomes. For decisions that affect you or others, your reasoning is the final word.

AI ethics — the practical version for engineers
Bias in, bias out
AI trained on biased historical data produces biased outputs. If historical hiring data favored certain groups, AI hiring tools will too — unless explicitly corrected.
Accountability gap
When AI makes a decision that harms someone, who's responsible? The engineer who built it, the company that deployed it, or the AI? This is unresolved — and you will face it.
Academic integrity
Using AI to complete work you didn't understand undermines your own education — and will surface the moment you're expected to defend it in person. Know what you submit.
Privacy and data
What you put into AI tools may be used to train future models. Don't put confidential client data, personal health info, or sensitive company information into public AI tools.
Transparency
When you use AI in professional work, disclose it appropriately. "I used AI to assist with this analysis" is honest. Presenting AI work as entirely your own is not.
Your actual edge
Your edge is not knowing more prompts. It's systems thinking + AI fluency + domain expertise + critical judgment. That combination is rare. That's what makes you genuinely valuable.
